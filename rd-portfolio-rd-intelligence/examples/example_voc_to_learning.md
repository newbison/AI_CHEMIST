# Example — Breathable Gentle-Removal Dressing

## VOC
Customer wants a more breathable, skin-friendly, long-wear adhesive dressing with less pain on removal.

## CTQs
- Breathability → MVTR
- Gentle removal → peel force, skin stripping, pain score
- Long wear → edge lift, wear simulation
- No residue → residue score

## Candidate Xs
- Adhesive coat weight
- Crosslinker level
- Adhesive chemistry
- Backing film thickness
- Backing film permeability
- Coating pattern

## Initial Hypothesis
Increasing adhesive coat weight increases peel adhesion but may reduce MVTR and increase pain on removal.

## Experiment
Coat weight × crosslinker screening DOE.

## Post-Experiment Learning Example
Within 25-55 gsm for adhesive family A on backing film B, coat weight significantly increased peel adhesion but did not significantly reduce MVTR using the current internal MVTR method. Crosslinker level above 0.5% reduced residue. Next experiment should focus on backing film permeability and film thickness.
