# Example — UV-Curable Hard Coat for Automotive Glazing

## VOC
Customer wants a UV-curable hard coat for polycarbonate automotive glazing with 9H pencil hardness, >90% transparency at 550nm, and 5-year outdoor weatherability.

## CTQs
- Hardness → pencil hardness (ASTM D3363)
- Transparency → %T at 550nm
- Weatherability → QUV ΔE after 5000h
- Adhesion → cross-hatch (ASTM D3359)

## Candidate Xs
- Oligomer type and molecular weight
- Silicone content
- UV dose
- Photoinitiator concentration

## Initial Hypothesis
Increasing silicone content improves weatherability but reduces crosslink density, creating a hardness vs. weatherability tradeoff.

## Experiment
Oligomer type × silicone content × UV dose screening DOE.

## Post-Experiment Learning Example
Within 10-30% silicone content for acrylate oligomer A, silicone content significantly improved QUV ΔE but reduced pencil hardness from 9H to 6H. Photoinitiator concentration above 2% reduced yellowing index. Next experiment should focus on dual-cure (UV + thermal) to recover hardness while maintaining weatherability.
