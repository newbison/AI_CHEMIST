# Evidence Level Rubric for Patent X-Y Relationships

## Level 5 — Controlled Comparative Evidence
- Comparative examples isolate one X.
- Same test method and conditions.
- Y changes clearly with X.
- Strongest patent-derived relationship evidence.

## Level 4 — Multi-Level Experimental Trend
- Table or examples show multiple X levels.
- Y changes directionally.
- Some confounding may exist but trend is visible.

## Level 3 — Example-Supported Relationship
- One or several examples contain X and Y data.
- Relationship is plausible but not isolated.

## Level 2 — Text-Asserted Relationship
- Detailed description states that X improves Y.
- No clear supporting example or table.

## Level 1 — Claim-Based Relationship
- Claim includes feature and benefit.
- No experimental support.
- Use only as low-confidence signal.

## Level 0 — Speculation
- No textual or data support.
- Do not include as a relationship.
