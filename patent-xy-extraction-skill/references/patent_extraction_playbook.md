# Patent Extraction Playbook

## Why Patents Are Difficult

Patents are written for legal protection, not for R&D clarity. The same technical variable may appear under different names, in different sections, and at different levels of specificity.

## Practical Strategy

Use three lenses:

```text
Legal lens — what is claimed?
Technical lens — what is actually taught?
Experimental lens — what was actually tested?
```

The experimental lens is usually strongest for X-Y extraction. The legal lens is strongest for IP risk signals but weakest for performance proof.

## Extraction Heuristics

1. Start with examples and tables, not the abstract.
2. Use claims to identify mandatory features, not causal proof.
3. Use comparative examples to identify causal contrast.
4. Treat long material lists as possibility space, not preferred design.
5. Normalize terms only after preserving original wording.
6. Never compare Y values without checking methods.
7. Always record confounders.
8. Convert relationship findings into DOE hypotheses, not final conclusions.
